## XII. TRANSLATION PRINCIPLES

### Pronouns for Deity
- **Always capitalize** pronouns referring to God, Christ, or the Holy Spirit
- Use archaic forms in prayers and hymns: Thou, Thy, Thee, Thine

### Tone and Register
- **Liturgical/Hymnic sections**: Use elevated, archaic language
- **Narrative sections**: Formal but accessible English
- **Homilies**: Balance between accessible and reverent

### Numbers
- Spell out numbers in narrative contexts: "fifteen years" not "15 years"
- Keep numerals for dates: "January 1, 379"
- Keep numerals for biblical references

### Consistency Notes
- "Church" is always capitalized when referring to the Body of Christ
- "Law" is capitalized when referring to Mosaic Law
- "Kingdom" is capitalized when referring to God's Kingdom
- Maintain distinction between "saint" (lower case in narrative) and "Saint" (title before name)

---

## XIII. SPECIAL NOTES

### Feast Titles
- Use "dominical feast" for Господњи празник (Lord's feast)
- Distinguish between "feast" (празник) and "commemoration" (спомен)

### Multiple Meanings
- **Служба** can mean:
  - "Service" (church service in general)
  - "Liturgy" (specifically the Divine Liturgy, or a particular liturgical service like "the Liturgy of Saint Basil")

### Regional Variations
- Prefer "Theophany" over "Epiphany" for Богојављење (consistent with Eastern Orthodox usage)
- Use "Most Holy Theotokos" rather than "Virgin Mary" in formal contexts
- "Nativity" preferred over "Christmas" for Божић
